About SAIL Fridays

What is SAIL?

SAIL (Student AI Literacy) Fridays are weekly drop-in sessions where faculty come together to explore AI integration in teaching and learning. These sessions focus on hands-on problem-solving: bring your challenge, and we'll workshop AI approaches together.

Why Context Matters

Prompts without context lose their value. That's why this library focuses on the full story: What problem were you solving? How did you approach it? What was the outcome?

Our Approach

We believe in learning by doing. SAIL Fridays combine experiential learning with a shared resource library, so you can both participate in live sessions and explore examples on your own time.

Rubric-Based Feedback Generation

üìã The Challenge

Providing detailed, rubric-aligned feedback on student assignments was time-consuming. Faculty needed a way to generate initial feedback that could be reviewed and personalized.

üí° The Approach

Developed a prompt that uses the course rubric as the evaluation framework. The AI generates structured feedback that faculty can then review, edit, and personalize before sending to students.

ü§ñ The Prompt

You are an expert teaching assistant helping to provide feedback on student work. 
Use the following rubric to evaluate this assignment:

[RUBRIC CRITERIA]

For each criterion, provide:
1. A specific strength observed
2. A specific area for improvement
3. A concrete suggestion for enhancement

Student submission:
[STUDENT WORK]

Format your feedback as a constructive, encouraging review that helps the 
student understand both what they did well and how to improve.

‚úÖ The Outcome

Faculty saved 40-60% of time on initial feedback generation while maintaining quality. The rubric-based approach ensured consistency across all student evaluations.

Adaptive Learning Path Creation

üìã The Challenge

Students in an analytics course had varying backgrounds. Some needed foundational review while others were ready for advanced topics. Creating individualized learning paths was impractical at scale.

üí° The Approach

Used AI to generate personalized learning paths based on student self-assessments and performance on diagnostic quizzes. Each path includes recommended readings, practice problems, and project ideas.

ü§ñ The Prompt

Create a personalized learning path for a student in [COURSE NAME] based on 
their profile:

Current knowledge level: [LEVEL]
Strengths: [LIST]
Areas needing improvement: [LIST]
Learning goals: [GOALS]

Generate a 4-week learning path that includes:
- Week-by-week learning objectives
- Specific readings from the course materials
- Practice exercises tailored to their needs
- A mini-project that applies their learning
- Checkpoints for self-assessment

Make the path challenging but achievable, with clear progression.

‚úÖ The Outcome

Students reported feeling more engaged and appropriately challenged. Course completion rates improved, and students appreciated the personalized approach to their learning journey.
